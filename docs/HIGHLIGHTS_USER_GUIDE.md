# Sports Highlight Channel - User Guide

## Overview

The Sports Highlight Channel lets you create custom YouTube playlists of sports highlights by describing what you want in natural language. Just tell us what you're looking for, and we'll search YouTube for the best matching videos and create a personalized playlist.

## Getting Started

### Basic Usage

1. **Enter your query** in the text box describing what you want
2. **Click "Build Playlist"** or use one of the quick presets
3. **Wait for the playlist to be created** (usually 10-30 seconds)
4. **Click "Play All on YouTube"** to watch your playlist

### Example Queries

- "NFL highlights from last night, 30 minutes"
- "NBA top plays from this week"
- "MLB bloopers from September 2024"
- "Kansas City Chiefs highlights from this season"
- "Sports upsets from last month"

## Tips for Better Results

1. **Be specific about the sport**: Include the sport name (NFL, NBA, MLB, NHL, etc.)
2. **Include a time period**: Specify when you want highlights from (last night, this week, specific date)
3. **Specify content type**: Tell us if you want highlights, bloopers, top plays, etc.
4. **Request a duration**: Ask for a specific length (15 minutes to 8 hours)
5. **Combine requests**: You can ask for multiple things (e.g., "NFL then MLB highlights")

## Features

### Query Builder

After your first successful query, a query builder will appear that lets you:
- **Select a sport** from popular options
- **Choose a date range** using date pickers
- **Adjust duration** with a slider (15 minutes to 8 hours)
- **Control content mix** between highlights and bloopers

### Playlist Features

- **Play All**: Opens the entire playlist on YouTube
- **Workday Mode**: Quick buttons for 1, 2, 4, or 8-hour playlists for background viewing
- **Explanation Panel**: See why certain videos were selected and how they were scored
- **Cache Status**: See if your playlist was served from cache (faster) or freshly generated

## Common Issues & Troubleshooting

### "No Videos Found"

**Possible causes:**
- Query is too specific or narrow
- Date range has no available content
- Sport/team name misspelled

**Solutions:**
- Try a broader search (e.g., "NFL highlights" instead of specific teams)
- Adjust or remove date filters
- Check spelling of sport/league names
- Try a different time period

### "Rate Limit Exceeded"

**What it means:** The service is experiencing high demand.

**Solutions:**
- Wait a few minutes and try again
- Use a cached playlist if available
- Try again during off-peak hours

### "Network Error"

**What it means:** Unable to connect to the service.

**Solutions:**
- Check your internet connection
- Try refreshing the page
- Disable VPN if using one
- Wait a moment and retry

### "Service Temporarily Unavailable"

**What it means:** The service has reached its daily API limit.

**Solutions:**
- Try again tomorrow
- Use a cached playlist if available
- Contact support if the issue persists

### Long Loading Times

**What it means:** The request is taking longer than usual (30+ seconds).

**Solutions:**
- Wait for the request to complete (it may still succeed)
- Click "Cancel Request" if you want to try a different query
- Try a simpler or more general query
- Check your internet connection

## FAQ

**Q: Can I download videos?**  
A: No. We only provide links to YouTube videos. You must watch them on YouTube.

**Q: Why are some videos missing?**  
A: Videos may be removed by YouTube, set to private, or region-restricted. We have no control over this.

**Q: Can I save playlists?**  
A: Currently, playlists are not saved to your account. Bookmark the playlist URL to access it later.

**Q: How long are playlists cached?**  
A: Playlists are cached based on staleness logic. Recent events (< 2 days) refresh more often than older content.

**Q: Can I request specific teams or players?**  
A: Yes! Include team names or player names in your query. The more specific, the better.

**Q: What sports are supported?**  
A: Major sports (NFL, NBA, MLB, NHL, Soccer, Golf, F1) work best. Other sports may work but results vary.

**Q: Can I mix different sports in one playlist?**  
A: Yes! Try queries like "NFL highlights then NBA highlights, 1 hour total".
